# Code of Conduct

Veda adopts the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) with one Veda-specific clarification.

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in the Veda community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

- Be honest. Be specific. Be useful.
- Cite sources. Show your reasoning.
- Disagreement is welcome. Bad-faith argument, personal attacks, and harassment are not.
- Keep discussions focused on decisions, frameworks, and evidence — not identity.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discriminatory language, or personal attacks in issues, PRs, or any project space.
- Publishing others' private information (addresses, account numbers, holdings) without permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussions.
- Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Veda-specific note on disagreement

Veda is opinionated by design. Framework interpretations will conflict. A disagreement over whether Lynch would categorize NVIDIA as a Fast Grower or a Stalwart is a legitimate, welcome debate — provided it is grounded in sources and stays respectful. "You are wrong because [citation]" is fine. "You are wrong because you don't understand investing" is not.

Refusing to hedge is a project value, not a license for rudeness.

## Enforcement

Report violations by opening a private issue or emailing the maintainer listed in [CREDITS.md](CREDITS.md). Reports will be reviewed and acted on promptly. Possible responses:

1. Private warning with specific feedback.
2. Public warning.
3. Temporary ban from issues / PRs / discussions.
4. Permanent ban.

The maintainer's decision is final for v0.x. Once the project has multiple maintainers, enforcement decisions move to the maintainer group.

## Scope

This code applies to all project spaces: the GitHub repository (issues, PRs, discussions, wiki), and any public channel where someone is representing the project.
